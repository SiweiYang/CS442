16 Q16 (posted Mar 14, due March 28)

Language: Haskell.

Weight: 1.0

Write a complete Haskell program (including main) to list the \(N\) most common words in the standard input, where \(N\) is a positive integer given on the command line (a list of the command-line arguments is provided by System.Environment.getArgs, of type
IO [String]).

A word is nonempty and made up of the characters ’a’ through ’z’ and ’A’ through ’Z’, though an upper-case letter should not be distinguished from its lower-case equivalent for the purpose of counting. All other characters are considered word separators. The output should be \(N\) lines, each line containing the count of a word, followed by a space, followed by the word (in lower case), followed by ’\n’. The lines should be in non-increasing order by frequency; words of the same frequency should be listed in lexicographic order.

To submit: Q16.hs.
